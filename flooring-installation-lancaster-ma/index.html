FLOORING OPTIONS & PRICING

Luxury Vinyl Plank (LVP)

$3–$6/sq ft installed

Most popular choice for Lancaster homeowners. Waterproof, durable, looks like hardwood. Ideal for kitchens, bathrooms, basements, and main living areas. Installs over most existing subfloors.

Hardwood (Solid & Engineered)

$8–$14/sq ft installed

Best for living rooms and bedrooms. Adds resale value. Solid hardwood requires acclimation (3-5 days) before install. Engineered hardwood is more stable in New England's humidity swings.

Porcelain & Ceramic Tile

$6–$12/sq ft installed

Best for kitchens, bathrooms, and mudrooms. Extremely durable and waterproof. Large-format tile (24x24+) is popular in Lancaster renovations. Labor-intensive — tile work takes longer than LVP.

Laminate

$2–$5/sq ft installed

Budget-friendly hardwood look. Better suited for dry areas — not waterproof like LVP. Good for rental properties and secondary bedrooms where cost is the priority.

FLOORING IN LANCASTER MA

Lancaster homes range from antique colonials and farmhouses to mid-century ranches and newer construction. Each requires a different approach to flooring. Older homes often have original hardwood under carpet that's worth refinishing. Mid-century homes with slab-on-grade are better suited for LVP. Newer construction is a clean slate for whatever material fits the budget.

We handle full flooring projects — demo of existing floor, subfloor prep, installation, and trim. We also coordinate flooring as part of larger kitchen and bathroom remodels so materials match throughout the home.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How much does flooring installation cost in Lancaster MA?

Flooring installation in Lancaster ranges from $2-$5/sq ft for laminate to $8-$14/sq ft for hardwood. A typical 1,000 sq ft first floor runs $4,000-$10,000 depending on material and subfloor condition.

How long does flooring installation take in Lancaster?

A typical 1,000 sq ft install takes 2-4 days. Hardwood that needs to acclimate adds 3-5 days before install. Demo of old flooring adds 1 day. Large tile projects take longer due to set and cure time.

LVP or hardwood — which is better for a Lancaster home?

LVP is better for areas with moisture or temperature swings — kitchens, mudrooms, basements. Hardwood is better for living rooms and bedrooms where warmth and resale value matter. Many Lancaster homeowners use both: hardwood in main living areas, LVP in kitchens and finished basements.

Do you coordinate flooring with kitchen remodels in Lancaster?

Yes. We regularly coordinate flooring installation as part of larger kitchen or whole-home remodels. Installing flooring before cabinets vs. after affects the total cost and look — we'll advise on the right sequence for your project.
