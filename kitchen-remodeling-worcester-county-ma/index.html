What a Kitchen Remodel Includes

Every Maverick City Builders kitchen project is scoped in writing before a single tool comes out. Here is what a standard full kitchen remodel covers:

Demolition & Prep

Cabinet removal, flooring demo, drywall repair, plumbing and electrical rough-in updates. All debris hauled and disposed of — your driveway stays clean.

Cabinets & Layout

Semi-custom and custom cabinet installation, island additions, peninsula builds, and pantry buildouts. We work with your existing layout or redesign the floor plan entirely.

Countertops & Backsplash

Quartz, granite, butcher block, and laminate countertops. Full tile backsplash installation. Undermount and drop-in sink cutouts. We template and install — no subcontracting the stone work.

Plumbing & Electrical

Licensed plumbing and electrical subcontractors we have worked with for years. Outlet upgrades to meet current code, dedicated circuits for appliances, new fixtures and under-cabinet lighting.

Flooring

LVP, hardwood, and tile installation. Subfloor leveling and moisture barrier when needed. We match new flooring to adjacent rooms when the project calls for it.

Permits & Inspections

We pull every required permit and schedule all inspections with your town's building department. You never have to call the building inspector yourself.

Towns We Serve in Worcester County

Maverick City Builders takes kitchen remodeling projects throughout Worcester County and into the MetroWest corridor. Each town has its own housing character and building department — here is what we see most often:

Lancaster

Our home base. Farmhouse colonials and split-levels. Strong demand for open-concept kitchen builds and cabinet upgrades in the older stock along Seven Bridge Road and Neck Road.

Sterling

Hudson

Clinton

Older New England housing stock with galley and U-shape kitchens. We see frequent requests for island additions and cabinet layout reconfiguration to open up the floor plan.

Marlborough

Rapidly appreciating market drawing MetroWest buyers. Updated kitchens have strong ROI here. Mix of 1970s–1990s colonials and newer townhomes — both segments active.

Worcester

Largest city in the county. High volume of multi-family and investor work alongside single-family renovations. Permit office at Worcester City Hall — we handle all filings.
