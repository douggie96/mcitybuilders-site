    Carpentry FAQs

    How much does custom carpentry cost in Massachusetts?

    A standard built-in bookcase or trim package typically runs $3,000 to $12,000 depending on materials, size, and detail level. A full first-floor trim refresh on a 1,800-square-foot home runs $8,000 to $20,000. We provide fixed-price quotes before any work begins.

    Do you do paint-grade or stain-grade carpentry?

    Both. Paint-grade typically uses poplar or MDF and is finished on-site with primer and paint. Stain-grade uses hardwoods like oak, maple, cherry, or walnut and is finished with stain and a clear topcoat. We help you choose based on look, budget, and how the piece will be used.

    Can you match existing trim and millwork in older homes?

    Yes. We work with several local mills that can custom-profile any historic trim pattern, and our crew has years of experience matching period detail on 1700s, 1800s, and early 1900s Massachusetts homes.

    How long does a built-in or custom carpentry project take?

    A single built-in bookcase or trim package typically takes 1 to 2 weeks. A full first-floor trim refresh runs 2 to 4 weeks depending on scope. We give a written timeline before we start.

    Do you handle the painting and finishing?

    Yes. We can deliver carpentry primed and ready for your painter, fully painted on-site, or stained and topcoated. Most clients prefer the all-in-one service so they have one point of contact and one written warranty.

    Are you licensed and insured?

    Yes. Maverick City Builders carries a Massachusetts Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) registration and a Construction Supervisor License (CSL), plus full general liability and workers compensation insurance.
