ABOUT BOXBOROUGH — WHAT THE HOUSING STOCK LOOKS LIKE

Boxborough is a small Middlesex County town of about 5,400 residents, bordered by Acton to the east, Stow to the south, Harvard to the west, and Littleton to the north. The town's character is defined by heavy tree cover, low density, and a predominantly residential population — there is minimal commercial development. Most of the housing stock was built in waves from the 1970s through the early 2000s as the Route 495 corridor attracted families priced out of eastern Middlesex County communities.

Boxborough Center along Middle Road is the town's civic core, with Town Hall, the public library, and a small cluster of services. The residential neighborhoods extend outward in all directions — Whitcomb Avenue, Apple Hill Road, Burroughs Road, and the developments off Flagg Hill Road are the primary clusters where MCB does the majority of our Boxborough work. Most homes sit on half-acre to two-acre heavily wooded lots, giving the town a secluded feel even though Boston's Route 128 tech corridor is only 25–30 minutes away.

The housing stock in Boxborough is heavily weighted toward colonial and contemporary styles from the 1970s through the 1990s. Original finishes — oak kitchen cabinets, laminate countertops, fiberglass bath surrounds, and hollow-core interior doors — are common in homes that have not been recently renovated. These are high-ROI upgrade targets: buyers comparing Boxborough against Acton and Concord expect updated kitchens and baths, and homes that have them move significantly faster in the 01719 market.

Permits for work in Boxborough are filed at the Boxborough Building Department at Town Hall on Middle Road. As a small town, Boxborough's building department processes residential permits efficiently — standard kitchen, bathroom, and deck permits are typically approved in 1–2 weeks. MCB handles all permit applications and inspections for Boxborough projects.
