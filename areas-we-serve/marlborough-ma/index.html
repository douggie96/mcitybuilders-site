ABOUT MARLBOROUGH — WHAT THE HOUSING STOCK LOOKS LIKE

Marlborough is a city of about 40,000 at the intersection of Routes 20, 85, and 495 in MetroWest. The housing stock breaks into three main types: 1970s–1990s colonial subdivisions in established neighborhoods like French Hill, Hayden Heights, and Hayden Falls; mid-century ranches and split-levels in the older neighborhoods near downtown Main Street and Hosmer Street; and newer construction in developments near Williams Lake Road and the 495 interchange.

The colonial subdivisions — which make up the majority of Marlborough's housing stock — were built with standard 1980s finishes: oak cabinets, laminate countertops, fiberglass tub surrounds, and single-vanity bathrooms. These homes have strong bones and good layouts, but the finishes are now 30–40 years old. That's exactly where we operate: upgrading the finishes to match the home's actual value in today's market.

Marlborough's 01752 ZIP benefits from strong Route 495 commuter demand — buyers heading to Boston, Framingham, Worcester, and the Route 128 tech corridor. This demand makes remodeling ROI in Marlborough stronger than comparable Worcester County towns. Updated kitchens and bathrooms consistently shorten time on market and support higher sale prices in this market.

Permits are filed with the Marlborough Building Department at City Hall, 140 Main Street. Standard residential permits — kitchen remodeling, bathroom remodeling, deck building — are typically approved in 1–2 weeks. We handle all permit applications and schedule all required inspections as part of every project.
