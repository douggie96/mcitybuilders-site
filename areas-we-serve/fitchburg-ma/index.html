FITCHBURG MA — TRIPLE-DECKERS, MULTIFAMILY, AND OLDER HOUSING REHAB

Fitchburg is a city of about 43,000 in northern Worcester County, 14 miles north of Lancaster via I-190 or Route 2. The city's industrial history — paper mills, machinery manufacturing, and Fitchburg State University as a major employer — shaped a diverse housing stock that ranges from working-class triple-deckers and two-family homes in the downtown Rollstone, Cleghorn, and South Fitchburg neighborhoods, to post-war single-family capes and colonials in Upper Common and Laurel Hill, to larger Victorian-era homes in the better-preserved streets off Main and Water streets. No two neighborhoods in Fitchburg have the same remodeling profile, and we work in all of them.

Triple-decker and multifamily renovation is one of the most common work types we handle in Fitchburg. The city's investor community is active, and the triple-decker stock — late 19th and early 20th century wood-frame three-family homes — is a staple of the Fitchburg rental market. Landlords and property investors typically need unit-turn kitchen and bathroom work completed between tenancies: new cabinets and countertops in the kitchen, a full bathroom gut in the unit's main bath, new flooring, and paint throughout. These projects move on tight timelines and require a contractor who can pull the permits quickly, coordinate the inspections, and finish on schedule. We do this work regularly in Fitchburg alongside neighboring Leominster and Lancaster. For bathroom line renovations — where all three stacked units need the same bath work — we phase the schedule so plumbing rough work in all three units is inspected together, reducing the total permit cycle. Each unit still requires its own permit from the Fitchburg Building Department.

Single-family renovation in Fitchburg's older neighborhoods is a different scope. The 1950s and 1960s capes and colonials in Upper Common and the streets off Electric Avenue typically have original kitchens with metal or painted wood cabinets, laminate countertops, and linoleum floors — all of it functional but dated by two decades at minimum. Bathroom tile in these homes is often original 4-inch ceramic in period colors with a mortar bed that adds weight but provides a good substrate for new tile once the old is removed. We see galvanized supply lines in homes built before 1965 that should be replaced whenever the walls are open — we price this as part of the scope rather than discovering it mid-project.

Building permits for Fitchburg projects are filed with the Fitchburg Building Department. Residential permit approval runs 1 to 2 weeks for most kitchen and bathroom work. Multi-unit projects require a permit per unit. Maverick City Builders handles all permit applications and inspections for every project in Fitchburg as a standard part of our scope — homeowners and landlords never deal with the building department directly.

RECENT PROJECTS IN FITCHBURG MA

Two-unit bathroom line renovation — Rollstone triple-decker

Fitchburg investor with a triple-decker off Rollstone Street — two of the three units needed bathroom gut work between leases. Same scope in each unit: demo original tile over mortar bed, Schluter waterproofing system on floor and shower surround, 12x24 porcelain tile in a neutral grey, new vanity with undermount sink, elongated toilet, exhaust fan, and LED light fixture. Fitchburg Building Department permits for each unit, plumbing rough inspections for both units coordinated on the same day to simplify the schedule. Both units completed in 22 working days total. Landlord had new tenants in place within a week of final inspection.

Triple-decker kitchen refresh — investor unit-turn, Cleghorn neighborhood

First-floor unit of a 1908 triple-decker in the Cleghorn neighborhood, vacant for unit-turn. The kitchen had original painted cabinets, a cast iron sink, and exposed galvanized supply lines that were corroding at the joints. We replaced the galvanized lines with copper while the walls were open, installed semi-custom shaker cabinets in white, quartz countertops, a new undermount sink and faucet, and LVP flooring over the existing subfloor. Painted the unit throughout. Fitchburg Building Department permit for the plumbing. Three-week project from permit to final walkthrough. The landlord's rental listing showed the updated kitchen and the unit leased within four days.
