ABOUT STOW — WHAT THE HOUSING STOCK LOOKS LIKE

Stow is a rural-to-suburban town of about 7,000 residents in eastern Worcester County, situated along Routes 62 and 117 between Hudson to the west and Acton and Boxborough to the east. The town has no traditional downtown retail district — it is primarily residential, with most commercial activity along Great Road (Route 117) and Boxborough Road. This character shapes the housing stock and the remodeling work we typically do there.

The majority of Stow's homes were built between 1955 and 1995 — a mix of capes, ranches, split-levels, and colonials on one-to-three-acre lots. Stow Center along Route 62, the developments off Hudson Road near the Hudson town line, and the Great Road corridor near Gleasondale are the three primary residential clusters we serve most frequently. Many properties in Stow are on private wells and private septic systems, which adds a step to bathroom and kitchen remodeling work — we always flag plumbing conditions that affect drain and supply work scope during the initial estimate.

Stow's housing market benefits from its position between Hudson (with its direct Route 85 and Route 62 access) and Acton (with commuter rail on the Fitchburg Line). Buyers looking at the 01775 ZIP code are typically comparison shopping against Acton, Boxborough, and Hudson — updated kitchens and bathrooms are the difference between a 14-day and a 60-day sale in this market. MCB has completed kitchen and bathroom projects throughout Stow and can provide references from homeowners in the town.

Permits for remodeling projects in Stow are filed at the Stow Building Department at Town Hall on Great Road. Standard residential building permits for kitchen remodeling, bathroom remodeling, and deck construction are typically reviewed in 1–3 weeks. We handle all applications, schedule all required inspections, and close permits after final approval — Stow homeowners do not need to visit the building department themselves.
