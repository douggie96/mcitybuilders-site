ABOUT SUDBURY — WHAT THE HOUSING STOCK LOOKS LIKE

Sudbury is a Middlesex County town of about 19,000 at the eastern edge of our service area, bordering Concord to the north, Stow to the northwest, Hudson and Marlborough to the west, and Framingham to the south. Sudbury is notable for its large lot sizes — most residential properties sit on one to three acres — and a housing stock that skews toward the 1950s through 1980s. The town has no central downtown district; the civic center is anchored by the building department and town services at 275 Old Lancaster Road.

Sudbury's three main residential communities each have a distinct character. Sudbury Center — the historic core along Concord Road and Old Sudbury Road — has a mix of older New England homes dating from the 19th century through the 1950s, along with some historic preservation context that affects exterior work. South Sudbury along Route 20 is the most commercially oriented area, with 1960s–1980s ranches and colonials clustered near the regional retail corridor. Nobscot, in western Sudbury near the Framingham and Hudson borders, has predominantly 1960s–1980s splits and colonials on larger lots and is where we see the highest concentration of gut renovation work.

The majority of Sudbury homes are on private septic systems and private wells — a higher percentage than most towns we serve. This affects bathroom and kitchen remodeling scope: supply line work, drain routing, and any project near a leach field area requires attention to existing system locations. MCB identifies septic and well conditions during the estimate walk-through and builds that context into the project plan before any work begins. We do not discover these constraints mid-project.

Permits for remodeling in Sudbury are filed through the Sudbury Building Department at 275 Old Lancaster Road. Sudbury uses the OpenGov ViewPoint Cloud online portal for permit applications and tracking — contractors can submit applications and monitor status digitally. Standard residential permits for kitchen remodeling, bathroom remodeling, and deck construction are typically reviewed in 1–3 weeks. MCB handles all permit applications and inspection scheduling for Sudbury projects.
