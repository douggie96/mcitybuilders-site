    Bathroom Remodeling in Worcester, MA

    Lancaster-based Maverick City Builders — about 30 minutes north of Worcester — handles full bathroom remodels across the second-largest city in Massachusetts, from triple-decker units in Vernon Hill, Main South, and Greendale, to Victorian multi-family conversions, to mid-century single-family bathrooms in Tatnuck and Burncoat, to larger custom rebuilds on the West Side.

    Why Worcester Homeowners Trust Maverick City Builders for Bathroom Remodels

    Worcester triple-decker bathrooms in Vernon Hill, Main South, and Greendale typically have tight 5x7 footprints with cast-iron stack-pipes that require careful demo and rough-plumbing planning.

    Victorian multi-family bathrooms often need full waterproofing rebuilds when converting from clawfoot tubs to walk-in showers — older lath-and-plaster walls behind tile demand a complete tear-out and proper waterproofing membrane.

    Mid-century West Side single-family master baths usually call for layout changes when converting tub-shower combos to walk-in showers; permits pulled at Worcester City Hall on Main Street through Worcester Inspectional Services Division.

    Worcester Bathroom Remodel FAQ

    How long does a Worcester bathroom remodel take?

    Typical mid-tier projects take 3 to 5 weeks, while a refresh runs 2 to 3 weeks. High-tier West Side custom rebuilds require 5 to 7 weeks.

    Do you pull permits through Worcester Inspectional Services?

    Yes, all permits are pulled by us at Worcester City Hall on Main Street. We also coordinate condo association approvals where required.

    Can you handle a tub-to-walk-in-shower conversion in a triple-decker unit?

    Yes, with proper rough-plumbing relocation, cast-iron stack assessment, and full waterproofing under tile. We ensure the new layout meets all modern code requirements.

    What about waterproofing under tile in Victorian multi-fams?

    Every shower and tub surround gets a waterproofing membrane (Schluter Kerdi or RedGard) over cement board before tile. This ensures a completely watertight enclosure.

    Can you work while other units in the triple-decker stay occupied?

    Yes, with dust containment, scheduled noisy work hours, and tenant communication coordination. We minimize disruption to the rest of the building.
