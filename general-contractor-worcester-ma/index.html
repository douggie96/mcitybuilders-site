HOW TO HIRE A CONTRACTOR IN WORCESTER — 5 THINGS TO CHECK

1. Verify HIC and CSL licenses

Massachusetts requires Home Improvement Contractors to hold an HIC license and Construction Supervisors to hold a CSL. Both are searchable at the MA Office of Consumer Affairs. Never hire without verifying both.

2. Confirm insurance — liability AND workers comp

Ask for a certificate of insurance naming you as additional insured. General liability covers property damage. Workers comp covers injuries on your property. If a contractor doesn't carry both, you're liable.

3. Get a written scope and payment schedule

A legitimate contractor provides a written contract with line-item scope, material specs, timeline milestones, and a payment schedule tied to progress — not a large upfront deposit.

4. Check who pulls permits

In Worcester, most remodeling work requires a permit from Inspectional Services at 25 Meade Street. A licensed contractor pulls the permit in their name. If they ask you to pull permits, that's a red flag — they may not be licensed.

5. Ask for local references from the past 12 months

Request two or three references from completed projects in Worcester or Worcester County within the past year. Call them. Ask about timeline, communication, and whether the final cost matched the estimate.
