DECK PERMITS IN MASSACHUSETTS

Any deck attached to a house in Massachusetts requires a building permit — no exceptions. Freestanding decks over 200 square feet also typically require permits. Maverick City Builders handles all permit applications through your town's building department.

Permit timelines in Worcester County vary by town: Lancaster and Clinton typically approve in 1–2 weeks. Worcester City can take 2–4 weeks. We account for permit lead time in every project schedule so your start date is realistic.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How long does it take to build a deck in Worcester County?

A standard 16x16 deck takes 4–7 days of active construction. Factor in 1–2 weeks for permit approval before work starts. Composite decking takes slightly longer than pressure-treated due to material handling requirements. Total project timeline from signed contract to finished deck: 3–5 weeks.

What deck brands does Maverick City Builders install?

We install Trex, TimberTech, Fiberon, and Azek composite decking, as well as traditional pressure-treated lumber decks. We'll recommend the right product for your budget, maintenance preference, and project scope.

Do you build second-story decks in Worcester County?

Yes. Second-story and elevated decks require engineered footings and are subject to more detailed permit review. We handle all engineering requirements and inspections. Second-story decks typically add $3,000–$8,000 to the project cost vs. ground-level due to structural requirements and added framing.
