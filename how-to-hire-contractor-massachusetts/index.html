How to Hire a Contractor in Massachusetts — What the Project Involves

Massachusetts law requires any contractor performing home improvement work over $1,000 to hold a valid Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) registration with the Office of Consumer Affairs and Business Regulation (OCABR). You can verify any contractor's HIC number online at the OCABR website in under 60 seconds. A valid registration means the contractor has passed a background check and is bonded — critical protection if a project dispute goes to the Massachusetts Contractor Arbitration Program.

For structural work — additions, framing, load-bearing wall removal, deck construction — the contractor or their on-site supervisor must also hold a Construction Supervisor License (CSL) issued by the Massachusetts Department of Public Safety. Ask for both the HIC registration number and the CSL number before signing any contract. Beyond license verification, always get written bids, ask for references from projects completed in the past 12 months, and confirm in writing that the contractor pulls all required permits. A contractor who suggests skipping permits to save time or money is a significant liability — unpermitted work can delay a home sale, void homeowner insurance claims, and result in costly demolition orders from your local building department.

Massachusetts How to Hire a Contractor FAQ

How do I verify a contractor's license in Massachusetts?

Go to the Massachusetts OCABR website and search the HIC registration database. For structural work, verify the CSL through the Massachusetts Department of Public Safety. Both searches are free and take under 60 seconds. Never hire a contractor who cannot provide their license number on request.

What is the difference between an HIC and a CSL in Massachusetts?

An HIC (Home Improvement Contractor) registration is required for any contractor doing home improvement work over $1,000. A CSL (Construction Supervisor License) is required for structural work — framing, load-bearing changes, additions, and deck construction. Some projects require only HIC; structural projects require both.

Should I get multiple bids before hiring a contractor in Massachusetts?

Yes — get at least two written bids for any project over $10,000. Compare scope, not just price. The lowest bid may exclude permit fees, disposal, or finishing work that another contractor includes. Ask each contractor to itemize their bid so you are comparing the same scope of work.

Who is responsible for pulling permits — me or the contractor?

The contractor is typically responsible for pulling permits in Massachusetts, and any reputable contractor will do so as part of their standard process. Confirm this in writing before signing. A contractor who asks you to pull your own permits or suggests skipping permits is a red flag — it shifts liability to you.
