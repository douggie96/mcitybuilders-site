    Lancaster Kitchen Remodel Realities

    Lancaster's housing eras each bring their own kitchen remodel challenges. The 1700s and 1800s farmhouses around the Town Common and along Main Street have low ceilings, post-and-beam framing that limits where you can drop a beam, hand-hewn floor joists with their own quirks, and original chimney mass that often takes up valuable kitchen real estate. The 1950s-1970s ranches and capes off Sterling Road, George Hill Road, and Old Common Road have undersized kitchens by modern standards, original 60-amp electrical panels that need upgrading before any modern appliance suite goes in, and knob-and-tube remnants. The 1990s-2010s colonials and contemporaries in the newer subdivisions have builder-grade cabinets that have hit the end of their useful life, single-bowl sinks too small for today's cookware, and laminate counters ready for quartz or natural stone replacement. We have remodeled all of them.

    Recent Lancaster Kitchen Project Examples

    Town Common antique farmhouse kitchen

    Full kitchen remodel in an 1820s farmhouse a short walk from Lancaster Town Common. Took down a non-load wall between the kitchen and original keeping room with a structural LVL where the chimney mass had been removed, installed custom inset shaker cabinets in cream paint, and integrated period-appropriate brass hardware. Quartz counters, restored original wide-plank pine flooring. Three-week timeline.

    Sterling Road mid-century cape full gut

    Full gut renovation of a tight 1962 cape kitchen. Removed the bearing wall between the kitchen and dining room with a structural LVL, opened the back wall with a new sliding door to the deck, installed a custom 8-foot island with seating for four, and brought the electrical panel up to current 200-amp service. Four-week timeline.

    Old Common Road colonial open-concept

    Custom kitchen and family-room open-concept renovation of a 1996 colonial. Full custom cabinet replacement in two-tone navy and white, quartz waterfall island, integrated paneled refrigerator and dishwasher, hidden pantry behind a flush cabinet wall. Five-week timeline.
