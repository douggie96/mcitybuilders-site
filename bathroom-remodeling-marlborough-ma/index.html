MARLBOROUGH BATHROOM SPECIFICS

Marlborough's housing stock — primarily 1970s–1990s colonials in subdivisions near Route 20, Route 85, and the 495 corridor — typically has one full bath and one half bath on the main level. The most common requests are tub-to-shower conversions (removing dated 1980s fiberglass tub surrounds and replacing with custom tiled walk-in showers) and double-vanity installations in master baths.

Permits in Marlborough are filed with the Building Department at City Hall on Main Street. Standard bathroom permits (plumbing + building) run $400–$800 and are typically approved in 1–2 weeks.

Marlborough's 01752 market sees strong buyer demand from Route 495 corridor commuters. Updated bathrooms are among the top decision factors for buyers in the $400k–$650k price range — a dated bathroom can cost you $15,000–$25,000 in negotiated concessions at closing.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How much does bathroom remodeling cost in Marlborough MA?

$8,000–$38,000 depending on scope. Most Marlborough projects fall in the $14,000–$24,000 range — a full hall bath gut renovation with new tile, vanity, toilet, and fixtures. Master bath renovations with walk-in showers push toward $38,000+.

What is the most popular bathroom upgrade in Marlborough?

Tub-to-shower conversion. Marlborough's 1980s–1990s colonials have dated fiberglass tub surrounds that homeowners want replaced with custom tiled walk-in showers. This single upgrade transforms the look of the space and is the top requested bathroom project in the 01752 ZIP code. Cost: $4,500–$9,500 depending on shower size and tile selection.

How long does a bathroom remodel take in Marlborough?

2–3 weeks for a standard hall bath. 3–5 weeks for a master bath gut renovation. Add 1–2 weeks upfront for permit approval. Tile work is the longest phase — large format tile requires additional set time before grout can be applied.
