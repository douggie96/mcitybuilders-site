Working in Sterling - What We Know About Local Homes

Sterling's residential neighborhoods span a wide range of construction eras. Homes along Chocksett Road and near Waushacum Pond tend to be 1960s-1980s ranches and colonials. Properties near Redemption Rock and Meetinghouse Hill Road include older farmhouses and newer 1990s-2000s construction. We've worked in all of them.

Common issues we find in Sterling bathrooms during demo:

  • Original cast iron or galvanized plumbing in 1960s-1970s builds that needs updating during replumb
  • Backer board behind tile never properly waterproofed - moisture damage behind walls on discovery
  • Subfloor rot beneath original tile in older tub surrounds
  • Electrical that meets older code but not modern GFCI requirements for wet areas
  • Undersized exhaust fans causing mold behind walls over time

We build contingency allowances into Sterling bathroom quotes to cover common discoveries. If we open a wall and find less than expected, the contingency comes back to you. If we find more, we discuss before spending it. No surprises.
